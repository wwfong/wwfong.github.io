A meaningful slice of my work at Abbey Road REDD was knowledge sharing across the music ecosystem. REDD was Europe's first dedicated music tech incubator and the innovation arm of Universal Music Group, the largest music label by market share, so the audience for this work spanned both directions: outward to the industry (founders, investors, technologists, artists) and inward to UMG leadership.

**Editorial pieces.**

- [Music at Long Distance: Online Creation and Collaboration Tools](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/music-at-long-distance-online-creation-and-collaboration-tools-from-abbey-road-red-2704), an analysis of the tools artists and producers were turning to during the early lockdown period.
- [Music at Long Distance: Artificial Intelligence Tools](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/music-at-long-distance-artificial-intelligence-tools-2709), the AI follow-up to the same shift.
- [A Historical Overview of Background Music](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/a-historical-overview-of-background-music-from-abbey-road-red-2833), a longer-form piece tracing background music from Muzak through to streaming-era functional audio.
- [The Positive Neuro-Physiological Effects of Music](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/the-positive-neuro-physiological-effects-of-music-from-abbey-road-reds-karim-fanous-2743), a deep-dive on the science by my colleague Karim Fanous, published in the same series.

**Monthly briefings to UMG leadership.** Each month I helped prepare a research briefing covering notable signals in audio technology, music AI, music tech investment, and adjacent shifts in consumer behaviour. The briefings were both an internal advisory product (executive context for strategic decisions) and a public artefact, with a public version published on the REDD blog. A selection:

**Programming the Playroom for Abbey Road's 90th Anniversary.** For Abbey Road Studios' 90th anniversary I helped organise [Abbey Road Amplify](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/announcing-abbey-road-amplify-our-90th-anniversary-festival-3057), the festival celebrating the studio's history and future. REDD's contribution was [the Playroom](https://www.abbeyroad.com/news/announcing-the-abbey-road-red-playroom-as-part-of-our-amplify-festival-3064), an experiential showcase of music technology where visitors could try out emerging audio tools, instruments, and immersive experiences hands-on. I scoped the Playroom programme, coordinated with the partner companies whose tools were on display, and worked with the broader event team on production logistics.
